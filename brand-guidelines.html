04 — Brand voice
Tone & language
NWA Ads speaks like a sharp local operator — not a tech startup, not a media conglomerate. Lead with the number. Name the place. State the insight.
Direct
Lead with the number, the place, the outcome. No preamble, no hedging.
"140,000 NWA impressions. 4 weeks. $1,320."
Local-first
Specific to NWA — streets, corridors, neighborhoods. Never generic "outdoor media."
"The I-49 board between Rogers and Bentonville."
Confident
State the insight, don't soften it. The data is real and the case is strong.
"Your X spend reaches NWA at a $222 CPM. This doesn't."
Tagline
"The Easiest Way to Advertise Locally"
Use in full on first appearance. Compact lockup below the wordmark. Never truncated or paraphrased.
05 — Usage rules
Do's & don'ts
These rules protect the brand's legibility and consistency across all touchpoints.
Do
Use Fraunces for all display numbers, headlines, and key statistics — especially CPM and impression figures
Don't
Mix orange and green as competing headline colors on the same surface — green dominates, orange accents
Do
Always use warm cream (#fffef9) as the page background — never pure white. The warmth is intentional
Don't
Place the icon mark smaller than 16px — the three-line structure becomes illegible below that threshold
Do
Pair the orange eyebrow label with the green Fraunces headline — this is the signature two-line entry point used across all pages
Don't
Use gradients, drop shadows, or glow effects — the flat palette is strong enough and gradients cheapen the brand
Do
Use sentence case for all body and label text. Reserve ALL CAPS only for short eyebrow labels and metadata strings (max 4 words)
Don't
Use the red (#991b1b) color in client-facing documents — it's reserved for internal dashboards and error states only
